About Resume

Remy

Never forget a special occasion again!

We are developing a mobile app that is built to fill the gaps in the market when it comes to gift suggestions, as well as more customizable reminders for your special occasions.

Olha Yastreb, Leticia Tientcheu, Melissa Mahon, (UX/UI Designers)

3 weeks

Miro, Figma, Google Suite, Trello

The Process

  • User Research
  • Definition & Synthesis
  • Ideation
  • User Testing + Outcomes
  • Prototyping
  • Conclusion

User Research

We Interviewed 6 potential users, conducted Survey via social media, and get 31 responses. On this phase, we clearly understand what users need, what changes they face when it comes to remembering special occasions and gift-giving, and what potential solutions they can provide, what they expect to see in those type of app. Base on interview results, we created a User persona and formed a Storyboarding. Next, we conduct a competitive analysis for direct and indirect competitors that help us with features prioritization and an idea of what gives our company an advantage over its competitors and strategies for expanding into a new market.

Definition & Synthesis

Our mobile app will be designed to achieve a more straightforward process for maintaining a special events calendar and corresponding gift-giving needs. We have observed that most products or services aren’t meeting these goals, which is causing busy users to spend more time than necessary on these tasks, possibly even forgetting gifts or special occasions. How might we improve our mobile app offering so that our customers are spending less time searching for gifts and managing commitments?

Ideation

My team and I start brainstorming using an I like, and I want, What if method regarding the app features. Using our insights from ideation, we prioritized the features to build by creating a matrix: Custom Notifications, Curated gift suggestion, Log of friend’s preferences, etc.

Prototyping

OThe low-fidelity wireframes design gave app a more visual and inviting appeal while allowing changes to be made throughout the design process. Transforming to Hi-fi prototype show our users and stakeholder how the actual app will look like: layout, animation, features, error messages, etc.

User Testing + Outcomes

Testing one of the essential parts of UX design. Conducting a usability test, we watch how users use our app, what challenges did they face, what was confusing, what they didn't know or were unable to find. We made a bunch of interactions base on usability test results. For example, we added coaching screens, change text font, added sharing options like phone, added list of upcoming events, etc.

Conclusion

This experience provided us with greater perspective on creating a useful and enjoyable App.
We learned that before creating an App, it is worth doing thorough research of the market to see if an App(s) exist(s) is available that might already be doing the same thing.
We had to scratch our first App design idea but were able to develop another that we believe is as exciting and useful.
